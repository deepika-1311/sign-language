INDIAN SIGN LANGUAGE CLASS

Indian Sign Language (ISL) plays a vital role in education as it serves as the primary mode of communication for many deaf and hard-of-hearing students across India. It provides them with equal opportunities to learn, express ideas, and interact in classrooms. By incorporating ISL into teaching methods, schools can break language barriers, enhance inclusivity, and improve the learning outcomes of deaf students. Teachers trained in ISL can communicate lessons more effectively, making education more accessible and engaging. Moreover, the use of ISL promotes social integration by allowing hearing and non-hearing students to interact and collaborate, fostering a sense of equality.
